About

I am a Lead Research Engineer at Trillium Technologies, where I work on applied AI solutions for the space sector. My work bridges machine learning and space technology, developing practical AI systems for industrial space and Earth observation applications. I am also Director of Studies in Physical Natural Sciences at Homerton College, University of Cambridge.

I have a long-standing interest in applying machine learning to scientific problems. Since 2018, I have been involved with the NASA Frontier Development Lab (FDL), an applied AI research accelerator that tackles challenges in space science. I initially joined as a researcher, and over several summers took on increasing responsibility, ultimately serving as a faculty member helping to guide the next generation of interdisciplinary researchers.

Prior to my work with Trillium, I was a Research Associate in particle physics at the University of Cambridge, where I searched for new fundamental particles using data from the ATLAS experiment the CERN Large Hadron Collider. I completed my doctorate in particle physics at the University of Oxford under the supervision of Prof. Alan Barr, and hold a first class Master's degree in Physics from the University of Manchester. As an undergraduate, I also served as a Junior Under Officer in the British Army's Universities Officer Training Corps.

NASA Frontier Development Lab

NASA Frontier Development Lab brings together researchers from academia and industry to apply AI to critical challenges in space science, in partnership with organisations including Google Cloud and SETI Institute.
